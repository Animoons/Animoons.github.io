Memories of an Ojibwe Childhood, 

naawaakamigook Maude Kegg, p.172-175


mewinzha long ago iwidi over there gabekanaansing portage lake gii-daayaang as we lived for a time baanimaa later go ah baanimaa later awegwen whoever iidog it seems

gaa-bi-dagoshinogwen someone must have arrived.  "wii-ayaamagad that it might be iwidi over there wii-zagaswe'idiwag they will probably get together to smoke and discuss," ikido s/he says nookomis my grandmother.  "wii-maajaa'idim someone will probably leave with one another," ikido s/he says. gaawiin no ninisidotawaasiin I don't understand her/him amanj uncertain ekidogwen what, that s/he must be or may be saying.  miish so then gaawiin no awiya someone inini man gii-ayaasiin s/he was not. inzhishenyiban my uncle who has gone on wiin s/he igo ah gii-ayaa s/he was.  gaawiin no dash then gii-mino-ayaasiin s/he was not well. niin I dash then ingii-onapinaag I hitched them up bebezhigooganzhiig horses. wii-bi-maajaa s/he wants to leave wewiib hurry gigizheb early, daybreak gaa-o-izhi-onapinagwaa so I had in such a way hitched them up bebezhigooganzhiig horses biboonodaabanensing to the little winter sled, gaa-bi-izhi-maajaayaang as we had in such a way left, maajidaabiiba'igoyaang we must have been driving the sled, ginwezh for a long time igo ah, amanj uncertain daso-diba'igan each, every measurement apii when, time, ginwezh for a long time igo ah bimiba'idiwaad that they that along, from place to place, one another that they ingiw those bebezhigoganzhiig horses, de-onagoshig until late in the evening, imaa there dagoshininaang that we arrive neyaashing on the point. bezhig-sh one then igo ah wiigiwaam birch home, magizhaa maybe gaye and wiigiwaam birch home, magizhaa maybe gaye and waakaa'igan cabin, gaawiin no sa ! go ah ingikendanziin I know it not, anishinaabeg people iwidi over there biindigeyaawanidiwaad as they together are entering.  mii so gaye and niin I ani-wiijiwag they away together a'aw that one nookomisiban my former grandmother. niwenda-zegis I am absoloutely afraid aaniin how, in what way waa-izhichigewaad as, that they are doing something in such a way.  awegwaagi look! ani-biindigeyaang as, that we are going on away entering imaa there, mii so iidog it seems a'aw that one bezhig one a'aw that one inini man mitig tree, stick dakonang as s/he holds it ezhi-ayinoo'amawaad as they in such a way pointed for the other(s) iniw the other(s) anishinaaben the other(s) person or people ji-namadabinid in order that the other(s) sit, imaa there ishkwaandeming at the door, ani-biindigeyaang as, that just we enter on away inoo-amaagooyaang as the other points for us gaye and niinawind just we iwidi over there ji-wawenabiyaang in order that we sit down.


iwidi over there inaabiyaan  as I look at such a place, inaabiyaan as I look iwidi over there, ate it is placed ayi'iing it is in a place imaa there, miish so then imaa there aya'aa it is. makoshtigwaan bear's head imaa there gii-atemagad that it was placed.  gii-wenda-mindido it was absolutely big a'aw that one makwa bear, zenibaansan ribbons akina all gii-wawezhi'aawinden it was decorated a'aw that one makwa bear.  miish so then imaa there onaaganan dishes gichi-niibowa a great many ezhi-ateg as in such a way they are in a place.  miish so then imaa there gaagiigidod that s/he speaks a'aw that akiwenzii elder man, gaawiin no dash then, and, but ingezikwendanziin I don't remember it aaniin how, in what way gaa-ikidod that s/he had spoken.  mii so dibishkoo similarly go ah awiiya someone nibod as s/he dies, maajaa'ind that the other(s) leave.  mii so gaa-izhichigewaad that is what they had done, mewinzha a long time ago.
